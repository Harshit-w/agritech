📊 Complete NPK Fertilizer Reference Guide
Nutrient Deficiency Signs Best Fertilizers Dosage Best Timing
Nitrogen (N) Yellowing old leaves, stunted growth, pale green colour Urea (46% N), Ammonium Nitrate (34%), DAP, CAN, FYM 20–30 kg N/ha in 2–3 splits Basal + top dress at tillering / branching
Phosphorus (P) Purple/red tint, poor roots, delayed maturity SSP (16% P₂O₅), DAP (46%), Rock Phosphate, Bone Meal 40–60 kg P₂O₅/ha at planting Incorporated at planting (immobile in soil)
Potassium (K) Brown leaf margins, weak stems, poor fruit quality MOP (60% K₂O), SOP (50%), Potassium Schoenite, Wood Ash 40–60 kg K₂O/ha split dose Basal + at flowering / fruit fill
Calcium (Ca) Tip burn, blossom end rot, distorted young leaves Gypsum, Calcium Nitrate, Agricultural Lime, Dolomite 20–40 kg Ca/ha or foliar 0.5% Pre-planting + foliar at fruit stage
Magnesium (Mg) Interveinal chlorosis (yellow between green veins) Epsom Salt (MgSO₄), Dolomite Lime, Kieserite 10–20 kg/ha or 0.5% foliar spray When deficiency appears, foliar fastest
Sulfur (S) Young leaves pale yellow, stunted growth SSP, Gypsum, Ammonium Sulfate, Elemental Sulfur 20–40 kg S/ha at planting Basal application
Zinc (Zn) Khaira disease, white bud, small leaves, bronze discoloration Zinc Sulfate (ZnSO₄ 21%), Chelated Zinc, EDTA-Zn 25 kg ZnSO₄/ha or 0.5% foliar Soil before planting + foliar if acute
